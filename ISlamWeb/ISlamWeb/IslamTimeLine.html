Islamic History Timeline

570 CE

Birth of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) was born in Mecca, marking the beginning of a significant era in human history.

576 CE

Death of Amina (Mother of Prophet Muhammad)

The Prophet (PBUH) became an orphan after the death of his mother and was cared for by his grandfather, Abdul Muttalib.

595 CE

Marriage to Khadijah (RA)

The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) married Khadijah (RA), a successful businesswoman who supported his mission.

610 CE

First Revelation

Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) received the first revelation in the Cave of Hira, initiating the message of Islam.

622 CE

Migration to Medina (Hijrah)

The Prophet and his followers migrated to Medina, marking the beginning of the Islamic calendar.

632 CE

Farewell Pilgrimage

Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) delivered his Farewell Sermon during his last pilgrimage, emphasizing justice, equality, and faith.

711 CE

Muslim Expansion into Spain

Tariq ibn Ziyad led the Muslim forces into Spain, laying the foundation for Islamic civilization in Al-Andalus.

1258 CE

Fall of Baghdad

The Mongols sacked Baghdad, leading to a significant cultural and intellectual decline in the Islamic world.

1453 CE

Fall of Constantinople

Sultan Mehmed II (Mehmed the Conqueror) captured Constantinople, marking the rise of the Ottoman Empire as a global power.

1492 CE

End of Muslim Rule in Spain

The fall of Granada marked the end of Muslim rule in Spain after centuries of flourishing Islamic civilization.

1924 CE

Abolition of the Ottoman Caliphate

The Ottoman Caliphate was formally abolished, ending a significant era in Islamic governance.
