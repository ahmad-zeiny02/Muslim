Timeline of Women's Rights in Islam

610 CE

Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) begins receiving revelations, emphasizing the spiritual equality of men and women in Islam.

622 CE

Islam grants women the right to own, inherit, and manage property independently – a groundbreaking move for that era.

624 CE

The Battle of Uhud demonstrates the role of women as caregivers and warriors, with women such as Nusaybah bint Ka'ab (RA) actively defending the Prophet (PBUH).

632 CE

During his final sermon, Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) emphasizes kind treatment and respect for women as a key principle of Islam.

7th Century CE

Islam institutionalizes the right of women to seek divorce (Khula), challenging unjust practices and ensuring fair treatment in marriages.

7th Century CE

Women are granted the right to testify in legal matters, ensuring their voice is heard in decision-making processes.

9th Century CE

Aisha (RA), the wife of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), becomes one of the most renowned scholars and narrators of Hadiths in Islamic history.

10th Century CE

Fatima al-Fihri, a Muslim woman, establishes the University of Al-Qarawiyyin in Morocco, recognized as the oldest continuously operating university in the world.

12th Century CE

Shuhda al-Katib, a female scholar, gains prominence as an authority in the fields of literature and Hadiths in Baghdad.

Modern Era

Women in Islam continue to play prominent roles in education, healthcare, politics, and community building worldwide.
